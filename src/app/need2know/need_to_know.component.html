Customs Declarations Notes

Items travelling across international borders are subject to international standards of Customs clearance including customs documentation. In each country, the Customs department is responsible for monitoring international borders and implementing its country's regulations. Transportation of goods across boarders will be subject to Customs regulations and requirements. Customers corporation in ensuring all the document requested from them by ZHE is availed when requested will ensure easy movement of goods.

Counterfeit Goods

ZHE security policy states that it is not allowed to transport counterfeit goods via the ZHE network. ZHE does not want to be associated with counterfeit goods and upon suspicion of such goods being imported, Customs will be informed. It is prohibited in many countries to introduce counterfeit goods and where proven the Customs authority will seize the goods and penalize the buyer.

ZHE may at its sole discretion refuse to carry other items listed below.
  • Bullion of any precious metal
  • Cash (legal tender - bank notes, currency notes and coins)
  • Complete firearms, ammunition, explosives / explosive devices
  • Human remains or ashes
  • Illegal goods, such as counterfeit goods, pornography and unlawful drugs
  • Live animals (including but not limited to mammals, reptiles, birds and fish)
  • Loose lithium metal batteries
  • Loose precious and semi-precious stones (cut or un-cut, polished or un-polished)

Restricted Items

Hazardous material, dangerous, goods including but not limited to aerosols, flammable substances, dry ice, biological substances, UN classified dangerous goods and any goods specified as such under International Air Transport Association regulations (“IATA”), the Agreement on Dangerous Goods by Road ("ADR") or International Maritime Dangerous Goods ("IMDG") regulations or any related control sector or relevant association.
